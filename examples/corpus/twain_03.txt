Paris. I had a love for Paris that was deeper than reason. I cannot explain it. It is not the food, though the food is good. It is not the art, though the art is magnificent. It is something in the air, or perhaps in the coffee, which is exceptional.

We went to the Louvre. It took three days. I confess I was not able to appreciate all of it. There are approximately eleven thousand paintings in the Louvre, and I looked carefully at perhaps two dozen of them. The rest I walked past at a respectful speed, which seemed sufficient.

The Venus de Milo was there. She has no arms, which I have always found to be a great conversation starter. I stood before her for a long time. The guide said she was the finest statue in the world. I did not argue, though I thought she might benefit from some accessories.

Paris at night is something that must be seen. The boulevards are brilliant with gas lamps. The cafes are full of people who appear to have nothing to do and no intention of doing it. I envied them deeply.

We spent money in Paris. We spent money freely and without remorse. It is the only sensible response to Paris.
